Generative
Art

<by Gabriel>

Generative art is an innovative form of digital art that is created using algorithms and mathematical rules to produce unique, complex and abstract pieces. It has become increasingly popular in recent years due to the ease of access to powerful computer systems and software tools. The creation of generative art involves the programming of a system that is capable of producing artwork automatically, usually in a randomized or algorithmic manner. The artist designs and modifies the system to control the output, but the actual artwork generated is unpredictable and can result in an infinite number of variations. Generative art is not only a final result but also a process of creation. The artist can modify the algorithm and experiment with different settings to achieve the desired effect. The result is a collaboration between the artist and the algorithm, often resulting in unexpected and creative outcomes. One of the key features of generative art is that it is not static but rather it evolves over time.
